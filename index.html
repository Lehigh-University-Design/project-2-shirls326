How to make the perfect medium-rare ribeye steak

Savoring impeccably cooked ribeye steaks, tender and juicy, is the ultimate way to relish a delightful steak experience in the comfort of your home. Crafting this culinary delight is surprisingly easy!

This exceptional beef cut stands out as a personal favorite and, in my view, ranks among the most flavorful options available. No requirement for extravagant or unique ingredients—simply gather some steaks, seasonings, and a dollop of butter for a perfect culinary outcome!

Begin by selecting high-quality ribeye steaks, ensuring they are well-marbled for that perfect blend of tenderness and rich flavor. Allow the steaks to reach room temperature, a crucial step in achieving an even cook throughout. This simple yet effective preparation method enhances the natural flavors and ensures a succulent result. Next, generously season both sides of the ribeye steaks with a combination of kosher salt, freshly ground black pepper, and a hint of garlic powder. Feel free to experiment with your preferred herbs and spices to elevate the seasoning to your taste preferences. Preheat a cast-iron skillet or grill to a medium-high temperature, creating a searing surface that locks in the juices and imparts a delightful char. Place the ribeye steaks on the hot surface, allowing them to sear for a few minutes on each side until a beautiful golden-brown crust forms. For an extra layer of indulgence, add a generous dollop of butter to the skillet during the final minutes of cooking. The buttery infusion enhances the richness of the ribeye, creating a mouthwatering finish that is both savory and luxurious. Keep in mind that the key to achieving the perfect doneness lies in monitoring the internal temperature. Utilize a meat thermometer to ensure your ribeye steaks reach your desired level of doneness—whether it's a succulent medium-rare or a perfectly cooked medium. Once cooked to perfection, allow the ribeye steaks to rest for a few minutes, allowing the juices to redistribute and intensify the overall flavor. This crucial step ensures a juicy and tender bite with every mouthful. Serve your impeccably cooked ribeye steaks with your favorite side dishes or a simple garden salad for a well-rounded and satisfying meal. The joy of savoring these delectable ribeyes, prepared effortlessly in the comfort of your home, is an experience that will leave you longing for more—an exquisite journey for your taste buds that turns an ordinary day into a culinary celebration!

Directions:

  1. Pre-heat your oven to 275º Fahrenheit
  2. Take out the steak and pat dry steak with paper towels
  3. Add your seasonings of salt and pepper evenly to both sides
  4. When the oven is done pre-heating, attach the meat probe thermometer to the steak
  5. Put the steak on a wire rack/baking tray and place inside oven
  6. While steak is reaching an internal temperature of 110ºF, start to pre-heat your pan to a super high heat (this will help with getting an excellent crust!)
  7. When steak has reached internal temperature of 110ºF, take out the steak and pat the steak dry once again with a paper towel on both sides
  8. Throw some butter into your pan and add your steak to the pan
  9. Flip steak 2 minutes on each side
  10. Throw some butter and herb of choice (usually rosemary or thyme) in again to base
  11. Let the steak rest for 5 minutes
  12. Cut the steak up and enjoy!

Additional Tips and Tricks:

  • For an extra burst of flavor, marinate the ribeye steaks in your favorite marinade for at least 30 minutes before cooking.
  • Experiment with different herb and spice combinations in your seasoning for a personalized touch. Smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, or dried thyme can add a unique twist.
  • If grilling, preheat your grill to high heat for a few minutes before cooking to ensure a perfect sear.
  • Let the ribeye steaks rest on a cutting board after cooking to allow the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and flavorful result.
  • Consider using a meat rub or steak seasoning blend for added depth of flavor.
